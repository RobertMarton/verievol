Abstract

Scaling reinforcement learning for visual mathematical reasoning requires more than generating harder questions: as data volume grows, the reward labels themselves must remain reliable. Existing data pipelines scale supervision while trusting the labeller, and policy-side methods assume the underlying answers are already correct.

VeriEvol treats scaling as a verifiable data-construction problem and decouples two axes before any policy update: prompt difficulty, expanded by route-specific evolution operators, and answer reliability, enforced by offline hypothesis-test falsification. It combines a type-aware evolution module with HTV-Agent, a verifier that accepts an answer only after multi-source counter-evidence has failed to refute it.

On a five-benchmark visual-math suite, scaling evolved SFT data from 10K to 250K samples raises mean accuracy from 35.42 to 54.73. With backbone, SFT initialization, and GRPO recipe held fixed, VeriEvol adds a cumulative +3.88 over an un-evolved RL baseline, with +1.82 from evolved prompts and +2.06 from HTV-Agent verification.

VeriEvol Framework

Two controls scale independently: route-specific prompt evolution raises difficulty, while HTV-Agent verifies answers before they become reward signal.

Type-Aware Evolution

Seeds are routed by task type into operators for decomposition, rephrasing, constraints, multi-hop reasoning, inverse solving, and visual grounding.

HTV-Agent Verification

Multiple solver hypotheses are challenged by counter-evidence, programmatic checks, and visual checks before the answer is admitted.

Closed-Loop Scaling

Accepted samples form VeriEvol-SFT and VeriEvol-RL pools, then rollout-based difficulty feeds curriculum prioritization and seed refresh.
